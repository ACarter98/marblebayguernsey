Where Stone Meets Sea

A beautiful bay known for its unique marble-like rock formations, created by millions of years of geological processes.

Guernsey Attraction

Marble Bay, locally known as Le Pied du Mur, offers one of Guernsey's most striking geological displays. The bay takes its name from the distinctive rock formations that resemble marble, with swirling patterns of grey, white, and pink granite.

Geological Formation

The 'marble' effect is created by gneiss - metamorphic rock formed under intense heat and pressure over 2 billion years ago. The swirling patterns represent different mineral compositions that were twisted and folded during the rock's formation, creating natural artworks that rival any man-made marble.

The Beach

Beyond the geology, Marble Bay offers a pleasant sandy beach sheltered from prevailing winds. It's quieter than Guernsey's main tourist beaches, offering a more peaceful experience for those willing to seek it out.

Photography Hotspot

Photographers are drawn to the contrast between the smooth, patterned rocks and the rough Atlantic waves. The best shots are taken at low tide when the formations are fully exposed, or during storm conditions when waves crash dramatically against the rocks.
